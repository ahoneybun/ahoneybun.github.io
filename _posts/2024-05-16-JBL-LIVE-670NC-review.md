## Preface

I bought these headphones with my own money and JBL had no involvement with this review at all.

### Android experience

This device supports Google's [Quick Pair](https://www.androidauthority.com/google-fast-pair-2-3220791/) so connecting it to your phone for the first time is as easy as turning it on, putting it pairing mode and accepting the prompt on your phone.

[Audio controls on Android](/images/{{ page.url }}/Android-Music-Controls.png)

It also has Google Assistant support so you can press and hold the right ear to speak to Google. This was very useful for setting timers when cooking, send a message to someone or checking the weather.

Controling music is like most earbuds like the Pixel Buds Pro and Pixel Buds A-series:

- Single press for pausing and playing the music
- Double press for going to the next track
- Tripe press for going to the previous track and/or restarting the current track

There are also buttons for these same features (though you would hold them for 2s) though they share the buttons for volume control and play/pause.
